## Block 3 — Countryside (Days 8–13)
*Base: near Lacock / Bradford-on-Avon, Wiltshire — within 25–50 min of everything below*

### Day 8 — Drive to Base via Avebury
- [x ] Avebury Stone Circle — Europe's largest prehistoric stone circle; walk freely among the stones (free)
- [ x] Silbury Hill — roadside viewpoint; largest prehistoric man-made mound in Europe
- [x ] West Kennet Long Barrow — 5,500-year-old burial chamber you can walk inside
- [ x] Lunch at The Red Lion, Avebury — only pub in the world inside a stone circle
- [ x] Check into countryside cottage base; quiet first evening

### Day 9 — Stonehenge + Salisbury + Lacock
- [ x] Stonehenge — timed entry (book well ahead); inner circle walk-among option (~£50–55/person; very limited)
- [x ] Salisbury Cathedral — England's tallest spire; holds an original 1215 Magna Carta
- [ ] Lacock Abbey (National Trust) — 13th-century Augustinian convent; beautiful medieval cloisters
- [x ] Lacock village — entirely National Trust-owned; almost no modern signage; atmospheric evening wander

### Day 10 — Bath
- [x ] Roman Baths — extraordinary Roman preservation; book timed entry
- [ x] The Pump Room — Georgian coffee/lunch room adjacent to the Baths
- [x ] Royal Crescent — the most perfect Georgian terrace in England (free)
- [x ] The Circus — circular Georgian counterpart; 33 townhouses in a perfect ring (free)
- [ x] Pulteney Bridge + Parade Gardens — bridge with shops on both sides; photogenic weir
- [ x] Dinner in Bath

### Day 11 — Wells + Glastonbury
- [x ] Wells Cathedral — England's smallest city; extraordinary scissor arches inside (suggested donation)
- [x ] Vicars' Close — oldest complete medieval street in Europe (attached to the Cathedral)
- [x ] Glastonbury Tor — 20-min hike to ruined hilltop tower; views across the Somerset Levels (free)
- [x ] Glastonbury Abbey ruins — Arthurian legend; supposed burial of King Arthur and Guinevere
- [x ] Glastonbury High Street — alternative, colourful town; good food

### Day 12 — Gloucester Cathedral + Cotswolds Villages + Thornbury Castle
- [ ] Gloucester Cathedral — spectacular fan-vaulted cloisters and medieval stained glass (free entry)
- [x ] Bourton-on-the-Water — River Windrush runs through the village with low stone footbridges
- [ ] Bibury — Arlington Row; the most-photographed village scene in England
- [ ] Burford — working Cotswolds market town with a good high street and food
- [ x] Check into Thornbury Castle — England's only Tudor castle hotel (built 1511; Henry VIII stayed here)
- [x ] Dinner in the Thornbury Castle Tudor dining room

### Day 13 — Thornbury Morning + Depart for Scotland
- [x ] Leisurely castle breakfast; walled kitchen garden and vineyard walk
- [ x] **Travel Option A (Recommended):** Train LNER King's Cross → Edinburgh Waverley (4.5 hrs, scenic)
- [ ] **Travel Option B:** Fly Bristol → Edinburgh (~1.5 hr; Bristol Airport is 20 min from Thornbury)

## Block 4 — Scotland (Days 14–19)

### Day 14 — Edinburgh Old Town + Dalhousie Castle
- [ x] Edinburgh Castle — Crown Jewels of Scotland, Stone of Destiny, One o'Clock Gun
- [ x] The Royal Mile — walk the spine of the Old Town from castle to palace
- [ ] Greyfriars Kirkyard — one of Edinburgh's most atmospheric historic graveyards (free)
- [ x] Check into Dalhousie Castle — 13th-century castle ~9 miles south of Edinburgh
- [ x] Dinner in the Dalhousie Castle dungeon restaurant

### Day 15 — Edinburgh: Holyroodhouse + Arthur's Seat
- [x ] Dalhousie Castle falconry session (optional; book in advance)
- [x ] Palace of Holyroodhouse — monarch's official Scottish residence; Mary Queen of Scots lived here
- [ ] Scottish Parliament building tour (free on non-sitting days)
- [x ] Arthur's Seat hike — extinct volcano in Holyrood Park; panoramic views (~45 min to summit)
- [x ] Dinner in the Grassmarket or Canongate area of Edinburgh Old Town

### Day 16 — Highlands Transit: Stirling → Loch Lomond → Glencoe → Fort William
- [x ] Stirling Castle — Scotland's most important royal castle; Mary Queen of Scots crowned here aged 9 months
- [ ] Doune Castle (en route from Stirling) — Outlander and Monty Python filming location
- [x ] Loch Lomond — drive along the east shore; stop at Luss village
- [ x] Glencoe — Three Sisters viewpoint; one of the great landscape views in Britain
- [x ] Arrive Fort William; dinner at The Crannog (fresh seafood on Loch Linnhe, if bookable)

### Day 17 — Jacobite Steam Train
- [ ] Glenfinnan Viaduct viewpoint — 20-min walk up from car park to hillside overlook (time to watch the Jacobite cross below)
- [ ] Glenfinnan Monument (National Trust for Scotland) — where Bonnie Prince Charlie raised his standard in 1745
- [ ] Jacobite Steam Train — Fort William → Mallaig return; Scotland's most scenic railway (~£38–45/adult)
- [ ] Lunch in Mallaig — fresh langoustines, crab, smoked salmon from the fishing boats

### Day 18 — Flexible Day (Choose One Direction)
- [ ] **Option A — Isle of Skye:** Fairy Pools hike (turquoise mountain pools) + Old Man of Storr
- [ ] **Option B — Distillery Day:** Glengoyne (near Loch Lomond, unpeated Highland malt) or Auchentoshan (triple-distilled Lowland malt, near Glasgow)
- [x ] **Option C — Islay:** Fly Glasgow → Islay (Loganair, ~45 min); Laphroaig, Ardbeg, Lagavulin distilleries *(requires overnight stay and advance planning — see advisory in must.see.md)*

### Day 19 — Final Morning + Fly Home
- [ ] Final morning walk in Edinburgh or Glasgow before airport
- [ ] Fly Edinburgh (EDI) or Glasgow (GLA) → Seattle (SEA)
