Churchill · Westminster · High Tea & Darts · Harrods

Westminster → Mayfair → Knightsbridge · Split afternoon, regroup at Harrods

London ⚠ Book
▼
☀️ Morning — All Together
  • Churchill's War Rooms, King Charles Street SW1 (opens 9:30am) — The underground bunker from which Churchill directed WWII. Churchill Museum within. Genuinely atmospheric. Allow 1.5–2 hours. Book at iwm.org.uk
  • Westminster Abbey, Parliament Square (5-min walk) — Coronation church of England's monarchs since 1066; Poets' Corner; Tomb of the Unknown Warrior; medieval cloisters. Allow 2 hours. Book at westminster-abbey.org
  • Lunch near St James's Park or Piccadilly
🌅 Afternoon — The Family Splits
Mom + Daughters
  • Fortnum & Mason — The Diamond Jubilee Tea Salon, 181 Piccadilly W1. Three tiers: finger sandwiches, warm scones with clotted cream + jam, pastries, Fortnum's finest teas. ~£75–85/person. Allow 2 hours. Book months ahead at fortnumandmason.com — mention GF at booking.

Dad + Son
  • Flight Club Darts, Nova, Victoria SW1E — 15-min walk from Westminster. Social darts with electronic scoring, food and drinks. Fun at any skill level; perfect for a 14-year-old. Under-18s permitted with adult (verify current policy). Book at flightclubdarts.com.

  • Harrods, Brompton Road SW1 — 15-min walk from both venues via Knightsbridge. Allow 1–1.5 hours. Experience it rather than shop it: the food halls (excellent GF labeling), the spirits department (outstanding single malt selection), the toy hall, the sheer spectacle.

British Museum · Tower of London · Leadenhall · Globe

East & Central London · The big history day

London All Together ⚠ Book
▼
☀️ Morning
  • British Museum, Great Russell St WC1 — arrive at opening (10am). Rosetta Stone, Elgin Marbles, Egyptian mummies, Lewis Chessmen, Sutton Hoo helmet. The Great Court glass ceiling is architecturally extraordinary. Allow 2.5–3 hours. Free entry. GF options at the café or Museum Street nearby.

  • Walk or short bus along the South Bank to the Globe (~25 min via London Bridge, or Tube to Southwark)
  • Shakespeare's Globe Theatre, Bankside SE1 — Evening performance (~7:30pm): check shakespearesglobe.com for July 2026 programme. Groundling (yard standing) tickets are £5 — extraordinary under the open July sky. Gallery seats £25–45. Book 4–8 weeks ahead.
  • Alternative: Daytime Globe tour if a long evening is too much after this day
  • Borough Market (5-min walk from Globe) — outstanding GF dinner options

London → Avebury → Countryside Base

Pick up car · Drive via the prehistoric landscape

Wiltshire All Together
▼
☀️ Morning
  • Pick up rental car from London (central location) or Heathrow. Drive southwest: ~1.5 hours to Avebury.
🌅 Midday — Avebury & the Prehistoric Landscape
  • Avebury Stone Circle — Europe's largest prehistoric stone circle; the village is built inside it. Walk freely among the stones (no barriers, no timed entry). More atmospheric than Stonehenge for many visitors. National Trust; free to access the stones.
  • Silbury Hill — 5-min drive; roadside viewpoint. Largest prehistoric man-made mound in Europe (~2,400 BC). Mysterious and striking.
  • West Kennet Long Barrow — 5-min drive + short walk. A 5,500-year-old burial chamber you can actually walk inside. Extraordinary.
  • Lunch at The Red Lion, Avebury — the only pub in the world inside a stone circle; oldest thatched pub in Wiltshire
🌙 Afternoon / Evening
  • Drive to cottage base (~30–45 min). Check in. Grocery run (M&S or Waitrose for excellent GF options). Quiet first countryside evening.
9

Stonehenge + Salisbury Cathedral + Lacock Abbey

Wiltshire · Three of the best in one day

Wiltshire All Together ⚠ Book Early
▼
☀️ Morning — Stonehenge
  • Stonehenge, near Amesbury — timed entry required; book well ahead at english-heritage.org.uk. Morning light is best (monument faces northeast toward midsummer sunrise). Visitor Centre exhibits are excellent; shuttle to the stones. Allow 2–2.5 hours.
  • Inner Circle (Stone Circle Experience) — pre-dawn access, walk among the stones; ~£50–55/person; very limited. Book at the same time as your flights.
🌅 Midday — Salisbury
  • Salisbury Cathedral — 15-min drive. England's tallest spire (123m); holds one of the four surviving original Magna Carta documents (1215). The cathedral close is one of England's finest. Allow 1–1.5 hours.
🌙 Afternoon — Lacock
  • Lacock Abbey (National Trust) — 15-min drive. Used as Hogwarts classroom and corridor scenes across multiple HP films. NT family membership covers entry.
  • Lacock village — entirely National Trust-owned; almost no modern signage; also used in Downton Abbey and Pride & Prejudice. Walk it in the late afternoon when the day-trippers have left.
✅ English Heritage family membership (~£140/yr) covers Stonehenge + dozens of other sites. National Trust family membership (~£130/yr) covers Lacock Abbey.

Bath — Full Day

Roman Baths · Georgian architecture · The best city in England

Somerset All Together ⚠ Book
▼
☀️ Morning
  • Roman Baths — book timed entry at romanbaths.co.uk; July is peak. Allow 2 hours. Extraordinary Roman preservation; the Great Bath with its columns and steaming green water is genuinely dramatic.
  • The Pump Room (adjacent to Roman Baths) — morning coffee or lunch in Georgian splendour. GF-friendly. Try the spa water if you're feeling adventurous (warm, slightly mineral-flavoured).
🌅 Afternoon
  • Royal Crescent — the most perfect Georgian terrace in existence; 30 honey-stone townhouses sweeping in a curve. Free to walk and photograph. No. 1 Royal Crescent is a period museum inside.
  • The Circus — the circular counterpart to the Royal Crescent, 5-min walk; 33 townhouses forming a perfect circle.
  • Pulteney Bridge — one of only four bridges in the world with shops on both sides. The weir below it is exceptionally photogenic.
  • Parade Gardens — riverside gardens below Pulteney Bridge; perfect for a July afternoon rest.
🌙 Evening
  • Dinner in Bath — strong GF options throughout the city. Bath is a genuinely good food city. Return to cottage base (20–25 min).
11

Wells Cathedral + Glastonbury

Somerset · England's smallest city + Arthurian legend

Somerset All Together
▼
☀️ Morning — Wells
  • Wells Cathedral — England's smallest city has its most beautiful cathedral close. The scissor arches inside (added 1338 to stabilise the tower) are architecturally extraordinary. Allow 1.5 hours. Suggested donation entry.
  • Vicars' Close — attached to the Cathedral; the oldest complete medieval street in Europe, still occupied by Cathedral staff. 23 houses unchanged in structure since the 14th century.
  • Lunch in Wells or on the road to Glastonbury
🌅 Afternoon — Glastonbury
  • Glastonbury Tor — 20-min steep climb up the conical hill. Ruined St Michael's Tower at the summit. Views across the Somerset Levels are exceptional on a clear July day. National Trust; free. Allow 1 hour return.
  • Glastonbury Abbey ruins — below the Tor in the town. Arthurian legend: the supposed burial place of King Arthur and Guinevere. Haunting, romantic ruins in beautiful grounds. (~£10/adult, ~£6/child)
  • Glastonbury High Street — alternative, colourful, and an experience in itself. Good food and lunch options throughout.
🌙 Evening
  • Drive back to cottage base (~50 min). Calm evening in — this is a full day.

Gloucester Cathedral + Cotswolds Villages + Thornbury Castle

Hogwarts cloisters · Villages · Check into Tudor castle

Gloucestershire All Together ⚠ Book
▼
☀️ Morning — Gloucester Cathedral
  • Gloucester Cathedral — the fan-vaulted cloisters were used as Hogwarts corridors in Harry Potter films 1 & 2 (including the "troll in the dungeon" corridor). Free entry. The Great East Window is medieval stained glass. Allow 1–1.5 hours.
🌅 Afternoon — Cotswolds Villages
  • Pick a cluster (all 30–45 min from Gloucester):
    → Bourton-on-the-Water — the "Venice of the Cotswolds"; River Windrush runs through the village with low stone footbridges
    → Bibury — Arlington Row is the most-photographed village scene in England (William Morris called it "the most beautiful village in England")
    → Burford — working market town with an excellent high street and good food
🌙 Evening — Thornbury Castle
  • Check into Thornbury Castle — England's only Tudor castle hotel (built 1511; Henry VIII and Anne Boleyn stayed here). Castle grounds, walled garden, vineyard.
  • Dinner in the Tudor dining room. The treat evening — dress for it.
⚠️ Book Thornbury Castle 6–9 months in advance for July. thornburycastle.co.uk

Edinburgh — Castle + Royal Mile + Dalhousie Castle

Old Town · Greyfriars Kirkyard (HP) · Check into 13th-century castle

Scotland All Together ⚠ Book
▼

Edinburgh — Holyroodhouse + Arthur's Seat

Palace · Parliament · Extinct volcano hike

Scotland All Together
▼
☀️ Morning
  • Dalhousie falconry (optional, book in advance) — a memorable start before heading into the city
  • Palace of Holyroodhouse — the monarch's official Scottish residence; Mary Queen of Scots lived here; Bonnie Prince Charlie held court here. Audio-guided through spectacular state rooms. Book at rct.uk.

Jacobite Steam Train — Fort William to Mallaig

The Hogwarts Express crossing · Glenfinnan Viaduct · Mallaig seafood

Scotland All Together ⚠ Book Months Ahead
▼
☀️ Early Morning — Glenfinnan Viaduct Viewpoint First
  • Before boarding: drive to the Glenfinnan Viaduct viewpoint (15 min from Fort William). 20-min uphill walk from the car park to the hillside overlook — the elevated view of the 21-arch viaduct across the glen is where the iconic HP shot was filmed. Time the walk to watch the Jacobite cross below you (first departure ~10:15am from Fort William).
  • Glenfinnan Monument (National Trust for Scotland) — at the loch head, where Bonnie Prince Charlie raised his standard in 1745. Short walk; free.
🌅 All Day — The Jacobite
  • Jacobite Steam Train — Fort William → Mallaig (2 hrs 15 min each way). Scotland's most scenic railway line: lochs, glens, the Sound of Arisaig. The "Harry Potter train" crossing the Glenfinnan Viaduct. Runs May–October. Book months in advance at westcoastrailways.co.uk (~£38–45/adult return).
🌙 Mallaig
  • Lunch in Mallaig — a small fishing port at the end of the line. Fresh langoustines, crab, and smoked salmon straight from the boats. Excellent and reliably GF. The seafood here is as good as anywhere in Scotland.
  • Return on the afternoon Jacobite service, or a ScotRail service if the Jacobite return is sold out
⚠️ Jacobite tickets open approximately Feb/Mar and sell out fast for July. Book the moment they're available. westcoastrailways.co.uk

Harry Potter Filming Locations

Across England & Scotland — a trail for the whole family

London Scotland North England ⚠ Book Early
▼

The Harry Potter trail spans the length of Britain — from London studios and Oxford libraries to the Scottish Highlands. Plan the Warner Bros. Studio Tour as an anchor and build the rest of the trail into the route.

London
  • Warner Bros. Studio Tour (Leavesden) — The definitive HP experience. Great Hall, Diagon Alley sets, Hogwarts model, costumes & props. Allow a full day. Book immediately. (~£52/adult, ~£42/child)
  • Platform 9¾, King's Cross — Trolley photo op + HP shop. Go early morning on a weekday to beat the queue.
  • Leadenhall Market, City of London — Victorian iron-and-glass market used as Leaky Cauldron entrance in film 1. Free.
Oxford & Wiltshire
  • Bodleian Library, Oxford (Duke Humfrey's Library) — Used as Hogwarts library and infirmary across multiple films
  • Christ Church College, Oxford — Staircase and Great Hall inspired Hogwarts
  • Lacock Abbey, Wiltshire (National Trust) — Hogwarts classroom scenes across multiple films. NT family membership recommended.
Gloucester
  • Gloucester Cathedral — Fan-vaulted cloisters used as Hogwarts corridors in films 1 & 2. Free entry (donations welcome).
Yorkshire
  • The Shambles, York — Medieval overhanging street that inspired Diagon Alley. Free to walk; combine with York Minster and city walls.
  • Goathland Station (NYMR) — Used as Hogsmeade Station in film 1. Ride the North Yorkshire Moors Railway steam train from Pickering through Goathland to Whitby.
Northeast England
  • Alnwick Castle, Northumberland — Hogwarts exterior in films 1 & 2; flying lesson courtyard. Broomstick training sessions offered in summer. (~£17–18/adult)
  • Durham Cathedral — Cloisters & courtyard in films 1 & 2 including Hedwig release scene. Free entry.
Scotland
  • Glenfinnan Viaduct, Highlands — The Hogwarts Express crossing in films 2 & 3. Free viewpoint via 20-min uphill walk. Time visit to watch the Jacobite cross.
  • Jacobite Steam Train — Fort William to Mallaig across the viaduct. Runs May–Oct. Book months ahead. (~£38–45/adult return)
⚠️ Warner Bros. Studio Tour: book the moment your travel dates are confirmed — July slots fill fast.
✅ National Trust family membership (~£130–150/yr) covers Lacock Abbey plus many other stops on this trip.

Ted Lasso Filming Locations

Richmond upon Thames, southwest London

London Dad's Pick
▼

Ted Lasso was filmed almost entirely in the Richmond upon Thames borough of southwest London — a genuinely lovely area worth a half-day visit in its own right.

  • The Prince's Head pub (Richmond Green, TW9) — The actual Crown & Anchor. A functioning pub. Walk in, have a pint exactly where Ted and Beard sat.
  • Selhurst Park, Crystal Palace FC (SE25) — The real AFC Richmond. Stadium tours available at cpfc.co.uk. ~30 min from Richmond by train.
  • Richmond Green & riverside — Walking and street scenes filmed throughout the area.
  • Richmond Park — 2,500 acres of Royal Park with free-roaming red and fallow deer. Excellent family hike.
  • Richmond Hill viewpoint — The Thames bend from the Terrace Gardens — the only view in England protected by an Act of Parliament.
ℹ️ Richmond: District Line (end of line) or South Western Railway from London Waterloo. July evenings here are lovely — great spot for a calm dinner by the river.

Stonehenge

Wiltshire · One of the world's great prehistoric monuments

South England ⚠ Book Early
▼

Non-negotiable. About 90 min from London by car. July is peak season — timed entry books out well in advance. Book at english-heritage.org.uk.

  • Standard tickets view the stones from a circular path ~10–15m away. Visitor Centre has excellent exhibits + 360° immersive experience. (~£30–32/adult, ~£18–20/child)
  • Inner Circle (Stone Circle Experience) — pre-dawn or post-dusk access, walk among the stones. ~£50–55/person, very limited, books far in advance.
  • Pair with Salisbury Cathedral (15 min away) — holds one of the four original Magna Carta documents
  • Morning light is best; monument faces northeast toward midsummer sunrise
  • English Heritage family membership (~£140/yr) covers Stonehenge free + dozens of other sites
⚠️ July is the busiest month. Inner circle access is extremely limited. Book inner circle tickets at the same time as your flights.
🥃

Islay Distilleries

Island of Islay, Scotland · The whisky island

Scotland Dad's Pick
▼

Islay is home to 8 distilleries producing Scotland's most celebrated peaty single malts. A full-day distillery circuit — or ideally an overnight stay — is a highlight for whisky lovers. Ferry from Kennacraig (~2 hrs) or fly from Glasgow.

  • Laphroaig — Royal Warrant holder; intensely peaty. Excellent visitor centre.
  • Ardbeg — Award-winning; Old Kiln Café is one of the best lunch stops on the island
  • Lagavulin — Classic Islay malt; beautiful waterfront setting
  • Bowmore — Oldest Islay distillery (1779); in the main village
  • Bruichladdich — Progressive, very visitor-friendly; unpeated options available
  • Kilchoman — Smallest farm distillery; personal, intimate experience
  • Bunnahabhain — Remote and scenic; lighter style
  • Caol Ila — Stunning views across the sound to Jura
ℹ️ Distilled whisky is generally safe for celiacs — confirm with each distillery. Book tours ahead — July is busy on Islay.
⚠️ Islay requires at least 1 overnight stay. July evenings are long and beautiful on the island — worth staying for.
🏘

Lydiate, Merseyside

Near Liverpool · Father's old home

Northwest Dad's Pick
▼

A quiet semi-rural village 8 miles north of Liverpool. A personal stop — a chance to revisit the place Dad once called home, and to show the family where that chapter of the story happened.

  • Lydiate Abbey ruins (St Katherine's Chapel, 15th c.) — A scheduled ancient monument; freely accessible, atmospheric, and little-visited
  • The Scotch Piper Inn — Claimed to be Lancashire's oldest thatched pub (~1320). A genuine piece of living history.
  • Leeds and Liverpool Canal towpath — Pleasant walking along the canal near the village
ℹ️ Combine with a Liverpool day — Albert Dock, Liverpool Cathedral, The Beatles Story. Liverpool Lime Street is ~35 min by bus from Lydiate.

Castle Hotel Stays

England, Wales & Scotland — sleep where history was made

All Ages ⚠ Book Early
▼

Castle hotels anchor the trip at key emotional moments. July is peak season — book 6–9 months in advance.

  • Thornbury Castle (South Gloucestershire) — Only Tudor castle hotel in England (1511). Henry VIII & Anne Boleyn slept here. ~£195–450/night. thornburycastle.co.uk
  • Ruthin Castle (North Wales) — 13th-century Welsh castle; 30 acres; medieval banquet evenings. Family-friendly. ~£120–250/night. ruthincastle.co.uk
  • Langley Castle (Northumberland) — 14th-century fortified tower castle near Hadrian's Wall. Tower rooms with 7ft-thick walls. ~£120–280/night. langleycastle.com
  • Lumley Castle (County Durham) — 14th-century quadrangular castle; Black Knight medieval banquets; close to Durham Cathedral. Best value. ~£100–220/night. lumleycastle.com
  • Dalhousie Castle (near Edinburgh) — 13th-century Scottish castle; falconry school on site; dungeon restaurant. ~£120–300/night. dalhousiecastle.co.uk
  • Amberley Castle (West Sussex) — 900-year-old; portcullis, 12ft walls, 19 rooms. South Downs walks. ~£230–550/night. amberleycastle.co.uk
✅ Lumley Castle and Ruthin Castle offer the best value and are the most family-forward options on this list. Both offer memorable medieval banquet evenings.

Practical Notes

Everything to know before you go

🚗

Getting Around

7-seat rental MPV for a family of 5 with bags. Drive on the left. Roundabouts everywhere — yield to traffic already on them. Avoid central London driving (congestion charge + no parking). Use Tube & trains in the city. Consider one-way rental: pick up London, drop off Edinburgh.

🍞

Gluten Free in the UK

UK allergen law requires restaurants to disclose gluten on request. Good chains: Wagamama, Nando's, Zizzi, Prezzo, M&S Café. Supermarkets (M&S especially) have outstanding GF ranges — great for picnic lunches. Always notify castle hotels 48 hrs in advance. Distilled whisky is generally celiac-safe.

Memberships Worth Buying

☀️

July in Britain

Peak tourist season. Long daylight — Scotland stays light until ~10:30pm. Book everything well ahead. Scotland midges are active in July evenings; bring insect repellent for outdoor evenings. Weather is warm but bring a waterproof layer — always.

🚂

London Tips

Oyster card or contactless bank card works on all Tube, Overground, and Elizabeth Line services — no need to buy tickets. Daily fare cap applies automatically. East Coast Main Line (LNER) is excellent for London–Edinburgh (~4.5 hrs). Book advance train fares 16+ weeks out.
