Avebury + Prehistoric Landscape

July 1 · Stone circles, barrows, and ancient England

All Together
▼
☀️ Morning
  • Avebury Stone Circle — Europe's largest prehistoric stone circle; the village is built inside it. Walk freely among the stones (no barriers, no timed entry). National Trust; free.
  • Silbury Hill — 5-min drive; roadside viewpoint. Largest prehistoric man-made mound in Europe (~2,400 BC).
  • West Kennet Long Barrow — 5-min drive + 10-min walk. 5,500-year-old burial chamber you can walk inside. Free.
🍺 Lunch
  • The Red Lion, Avebury — the only pub in the world inside a stone circle.
🌅 Afternoon
  • Return to Bradford-on-Avon; quiet village wander along the River Avon; calm evening in.

Harry Potter Filming Locations

Across England & Scotland — a trail for the whole family

London Scotland North England ⚠ Book Early
▼

The Harry Potter trail spans the length of Britain — from London studios and Oxford libraries to the Scottish Highlands. Plan the Warner Bros. Studio Tour as an anchor and build the rest of the trail into the route.

London
  • Warner Bros. Studio Tour (Leavesden) — The definitive HP experience. Great Hall, Diagon Alley sets, Hogwarts model, costumes & props. Allow a full day. Book immediately. (~£52/adult, ~£42/child)
  • Platform 9¾, King's Cross — Trolley photo op + HP shop. Go early morning on a weekday to beat the queue.
  • Leadenhall Market, City of London — Victorian iron-and-glass market used as Leaky Cauldron entrance in film 1. Free.
Oxford & Wiltshire
  • Bodleian Library, Oxford (Duke Humfrey's Library) — Used as Hogwarts library and infirmary across multiple films
  • Christ Church College, Oxford — Staircase and Great Hall inspired Hogwarts
  • Lacock Abbey, Wiltshire (National Trust) — Hogwarts classroom scenes across multiple films. NT family membership recommended.
Gloucester
  • Gloucester Cathedral — Fan-vaulted cloisters used as Hogwarts corridors in films 1 & 2. Free entry (donations welcome).
Yorkshire
  • The Shambles, York — Medieval overhanging street that inspired Diagon Alley. Free to walk; combine with York Minster and city walls.
  • Goathland Station (NYMR) — Used as Hogsmeade Station in film 1. Ride the North Yorkshire Moors Railway steam train from Pickering through Goathland to Whitby.
Northeast England
  • Alnwick Castle, Northumberland — Hogwarts exterior in films 1 & 2; flying lesson courtyard. Broomstick training sessions offered in summer. (~£17–18/adult)
  • Durham Cathedral — Cloisters & courtyard in films 1 & 2 including Hedwig release scene. Free entry.
Scotland
  • Glenfinnan Viaduct, Highlands — The Hogwarts Express crossing in films 2 & 3. Free viewpoint via 20-min uphill walk. Time visit to watch the Jacobite cross.
  • Jacobite Steam Train — Fort William to Mallaig across the viaduct. Runs May–Oct. Book months ahead. (~£38–45/adult return)
⚠️ Warner Bros. Studio Tour: book the moment your travel dates are confirmed — July slots fill fast.
✅ National Trust family membership (~£130–150/yr) covers Lacock Abbey plus many other stops on this trip.

Ted Lasso Filming Locations

Stonehenge

Wiltshire · One of the world's great prehistoric monuments

South England ⚠ Book Early
▼

Non-negotiable. About 90 min from London by car. July is peak season — timed entry books out well in advance. Book at english-heritage.org.uk.

  • Standard tickets view the stones from a circular path ~10–15m away. Visitor Centre has excellent exhibits + 360° immersive experience. (~£30–32/adult, ~£18–20/child)
  • Inner Circle (Stone Circle Experience) — pre-dawn or post-dusk access, walk among the stones. ~£50–55/person, very limited, books far in advance.
  • Pair with Salisbury Cathedral (15 min away) — holds one of the four original Magna Carta documents
  • Morning light is best; monument faces northeast toward midsummer sunrise
  • English Heritage family membership (~£140/yr) covers Stonehenge free + dozens of other sites
⚠️ July is the busiest month. Inner circle access is extremely limited. Book inner circle tickets at the same time as your flights.

Islay Distilleries

Island of Islay, Scotland · The whisky island

Scotland Dad's Pick
▼

Islay is home to 8 distilleries producing Scotland's most celebrated peaty single malts. A full-day distillery circuit — or ideally an overnight stay — is a highlight for whisky lovers. Ferry from Kennacraig (~2 hrs) or fly from Glasgow.

  • Laphroaig — Royal Warrant holder; intensely peaty. Excellent visitor centre.
  • Ardbeg — Award-winning; Old Kiln Café is one of the best lunch stops on the island
  • Lagavulin — Classic Islay malt; beautiful waterfront setting
  • Bowmore — Oldest Islay distillery (1779); in the main village
  • Bruichladdich — Progressive, very visitor-friendly; unpeated options available
  • Kilchoman — Smallest farm distillery; personal, intimate experience
  • Bunnahabhain — Remote and scenic; lighter style
  • Caol Ila — Stunning views across the sound to Jura
ℹ️ Distilled whisky is generally safe for celiacs — confirm with each distillery. Book tours ahead — July is busy on Islay.
⚠️ Islay requires at least 1 overnight stay. July evenings are long and beautiful on the island — worth staying for.

Castle Hotel Stays

England, Wales & Scotland — sleep where history was made

All Ages ⚠ Book Early
▼

Castle hotels anchor the trip at key emotional moments. July is peak season — book 6–9 months in advance.

  • Thornbury Castle (South Gloucestershire) — Only Tudor castle hotel in England (1511). Henry VIII & Anne Boleyn slept here. ~£195–450/night. thornburycastle.co.uk
  • Ruthin Castle (North Wales) — 13th-century Welsh castle; 30 acres; medieval banquet evenings. Family-friendly. ~£120–250/night. ruthincastle.co.uk
  • Langley Castle (Northumberland) — 14th-century fortified tower castle near Hadrian's Wall. Tower rooms with 7ft-thick walls. ~£120–280/night. langleycastle.com
  • Lumley Castle (County Durham) — 14th-century quadrangular castle; Black Knight medieval banquets; close to Durham Cathedral. Best value. ~£100–220/night. lumleycastle.com
  • Dalhousie Castle (near Edinburgh) — 13th-century Scottish castle; falconry school on site; dungeon restaurant. ~£120–300/night. dalhousiecastle.co.uk
  • Amberley Castle (West Sussex) — 900-year-old; portcullis, 12ft walls, 19 rooms. South Downs walks. ~£230–550/night. amberleycastle.co.uk
✅ Lumley Castle and Ruthin Castle offer the best value and are the most family-forward options on this list. Both offer memorable medieval banquet evenings.

Practical Notes

Everything to know before you go

🚗

Getting Around

7-seat rental MPV for a family of 5 with bags. Drive on the left. Roundabouts everywhere — yield to traffic already on them. Avoid central London driving (congestion charge + no parking). Use Tube & trains in the city.

Two rentals: Rental 1 — pick up Heathrow June 28, drop off Bath Spa July 5. Rental 2 — pick up Edinburgh July 5, drop off Glasgow Airport July 11. No car needed in London.

🍞

Gluten Free in the UK

UK allergen law requires restaurants to disclose gluten on request. Good chains: Wagamama, Nando's, Zizzi, Prezzo, M&S Café. Supermarkets (M&S especially) have outstanding GF ranges — great for picnic lunches. Always notify castle hotels 48 hrs in advance. Distilled whisky is generally celiac-safe.

July in Britain

Peak tourist season. Long daylight — Scotland stays light until ~10:30pm. Book everything well ahead. Scotland midges are active in July evenings; bring insect repellent for outdoor evenings. Weather is warm but bring a waterproof layer — always.

🚂

London Tips

Oyster card or contactless bank card works on all Tube, Overground, and Elizabeth Line services — no need to buy tickets. Daily fare cap applies automatically.

Three train journeys to book: GWR Bath Spa → London Paddington · LNER King's Cross → Edinburgh Waverley (~4.5 hrs, scenic) · Avanti Glasgow Central → London Euston (~4.5 hrs). All: book 16+ weeks ahead for best advance fares.
