# Must See / Must Do

## Islay Distilleries (Island of Islay, Scotland)

Islay is the whisky island — home to some of Scotland's most iconic distilleries, known for peaty, smoky single malts. A half-day or full-day distillery tour circuit is a must for whisky lovers.

**Notable Islay Distilleries:**
- **Laphroaig** — Royal Warrant holder, intensely peaty, visitor center with tours
- **Ardbeg** — Award-winning, great café on site, Old Kiln Café for food
- **Lagavulin** — Classic Islay malt, beautiful waterfront location, featured in Parks & Recreation
- **Bowmore** — The oldest Islay distillery (1779), in the village of Bowmore
- **Bruichladdich** — Progressive Hebridean Distillery, unpeated options, very visitor-friendly
- **Kilchoman** — Farm distillery, smallest on Islay, very personal experience
- **Bunnahabhain** — Remote, scenic, lighter style, road trip to get there
- **Caol Ila** — Stunning views of the Paps of Jura across the sound

**Getting to Islay:**
- Ferry from Kennacraig (Kintyre peninsula) — ~2 hour crossing to Port Ellen or Port Askaig
- Or fly from Glasgow (short hop)

**Notes:**
- Best to book distillery tours in advance
- Islay requires at least 1 overnight stay to do it justice
- Food options are limited — plan around distillery cafés (Ardbeg's is excellent)
- Gluten note: whisky is distilled and generally safe for celiacs, but confirm with each distillery

## Ted Lasso Filming Locations (Richmond upon Thames, London)

Ted Lasso was filmed almost entirely in southwest London — specifically the **Richmond upon Thames** borough — and it's a genuinely lovely area to spend an afternoon.

### Key Locations

- **The Crown & Anchor Pub (The Prince's Head)**
  - Richmond Green, Richmond upon Thames TW9
  - The actual pub used as Ted and Beard's local. It's a functioning pub — walk in and have a pint exactly where they sat. Richmond Green is right outside.

- **Nelson Road / AFC Richmond Stadium (Selhurst Park)**
  - Whitehorse Lane, London SE25 — home of Crystal Palace FC
  - The exterior and interior of "AFC Richmond's ground" is Crystal Palace's actual stadium. Stadium tours available via cpfc.co.uk — check for availability.
  - Note: Selhurst Park is in south London, about 30 min from Richmond by car or train.

- **Richmond upon Thames generally**
  - Most walking/street scenes were filmed around Richmond. A wander through the area will surface recognizable spots.
  - **Richmond Park** — one of London's Royal Parks, 2,500 acres with free-roaming red and fallow deer. Outstanding for a family hike and a genuine Richmond experience.
  - **Richmond Hill viewpoint** — the only view in England protected by an Act of Parliament. The bend in the Thames from the Terrace Gardens is stunning.

**Getting there:** District Line (Richmond branch) from central London, or South Western Railway from London Waterloo.

**Notes:**
- Richmond is a very pleasant area independent of the Ted Lasso connection — good restaurants, a beautiful riverfront, and easy access to Richmond Park make it a strong half-day or full-day stop.
- Combine with Kew Gardens (Royal Botanic Gardens) nearby if time allows.

## Stonehenge (Wiltshire, England)

One of the world's great prehistoric monuments — non-negotiable for this trip.

- **Book timed entry tickets in advance** — sells out weeks ahead, especially in summer
- Located near Amesbury, ~90 min from London, easily combined with a Cotswolds or Bath day
- **Inner Circle Access** — special tickets available for early morning or evening access (walk among the stones); very limited, book as early as possible
- Pair with **Salisbury Cathedral** nearby — holds one of the four surviving original copies of the Magna Carta
- The **Visitor Centre** has good exhibits on how and why Stonehenge was built — worth the extra time

**Notes:**
- Standard tickets include shuttle to the stones; inner circle is a separate premium ticket
- Windy and exposed — bring layers regardless of season
- Gluten-friendly food available at the visitor centre café

## Harry Potter Filming Locations

A must for the kids — spread across England and Scotland.

### London
- **Warner Bros. Studio Tour — The Making of Harry Potter** (Leavesden, near Watford)
  - THE anchor HP experience — Great Hall, Diagon Alley sets, Hogwarts castle model, costumes, props
  - Book well in advance; allow a full day
  - ~25 min by train from London Euston to Watford, then shuttle
- **Platform 9¾** — King's Cross Station, London
  - Photo opportunity with trolley disappearing into the wall; official shop on site
- **Leadenhall Market** — City of London
  - Used as the entrance to the Leaky Cauldron in the films; beautiful Victorian covered market

### Oxford & the Cotswolds
- **Christ Church College, Oxford** — The staircase inspired Hogwarts; the Great Hall inspired (but didn't film) the Hogwarts dining hall
- **Bodleian Library, Oxford** (Duke Humfrey's Library) — used as Hogwarts library and infirmary
- **Lacock Abbey, Wiltshire** — used for various Hogwarts interior corridor scenes across multiple films

### Gloucester & the Southwest
- **Gloucester Cathedral** — cloisters used for Hogwarts corridors in films 1 & 2; the famous "troll in the dungeon" corridor

### Yorkshire
- **The Shambles, York** — the winding medieval street that inspired Diagon Alley
- **Goathland Station** (North Yorkshire Moors Railway) — used as Hogsmeade Station in film 1; steam trains still run

### Northeast England
- **Alnwick Castle, Northumberland** — exterior Hogwarts scenes in films 1 & 2; broomstick flying lessons offered to visitors; Poison Garden at Alnwick Gardens is a bonus

### Durham
- **Durham Cathedral** — used for Hogwarts exterior and courtyard scenes; cloisters appear in multiple films

### Scotland
- **Glenfinnan Viaduct, Highlands** — the Hogwarts Express crossing; iconic view from the hillside
  - The **Jacobite Steam Train** runs across it (Fort William to Mallaig) — book months in advance, seasonal (May–Oct)
  - Even without the train, the viaduct viewpoint is stunning

**Notes:**
- Warner Bros. Studio Tour should be booked the moment dates are confirmed — it sells out
- Alnwick and Durham can be combined into one day heading north
- The Jacobite train is a separate experience from just seeing the viaduct — both are worth it
