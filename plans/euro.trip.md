# Britain 2026 — Trip Brief

## Overview

A family of 5 (Father 57, Mother 44, Daughter 19, Daughter 14, Son 14) spending
approximately 19 days in the British Isles in July 2026. The trip is structured as:

- **~13 days** — Southern & Central England, Wales, Northwest, Northeast England
- **~6 days** — Scotland

Fly in: **SEA → LHR** (London Heathrow)
Fly out: **Edinburgh or London** (TBD — one-way car rental makes Edinburgh departure cleaner)
Transport: 7-seat rental MPV for countryside; Tube and trains within London

Travel philosophy: Rick Steves / Samantha Brown — thoughtful, experience-first,
not rushed. Calm evenings are non-negotiable. One great thing per day is enough.

---

## Must Haves (Non-Negotiable)

These anchor the itinerary. Everything else fits around them.

1. **Warner Bros. Studio Tour** (Leavesden, near London) — full day, book immediately
2. **Stonehenge** — timed entry required; inner circle if available
3. **Ted Lasso filming locations** — Richmond upon Thames (pub, park, riverside)
4. **Glenfinnan Viaduct + Jacobite Steam Train** — full day Fort William to Mallaig
5. **Alnwick Castle** — Hogwarts exterior, broomstick training
6. **A meaningful castle hotel stay** — at minimum one; ideally two or three
7. **Lydiate** — Father's old village near Liverpool; Lydiate Abbey, Scotch Piper pub
8. **Islay distilleries** — see advisory note below

## Proposed Route (South-Heavy Structure)

### Block 1 — London (Days 1–4)
Arrive, recover from jet lag. Base in or near the city.

- **Day 1:** Arrive LHR, settle in. Light evening walk — Richmond riverside, pub dinner.
  Ted Lasso location: The Prince's Head (the Crown & Anchor), Richmond Green.
- **Day 2:** Warner Bros. Studio Tour (Leavesden) — full day. Platform 9¾ at King's Cross
  on the way back. HP shop.
- **Day 3:** Central London — Tower of London, Westminster, Leadenhall Market
  (HP Leaky Cauldron entrance), Thames walk.
- **Day 4:** Richmond day — Richmond Park (deer, open space), Richmond Hill viewpoint,
  Kew Gardens optional. Leisurely Ted Lasso afternoon.

### Block 2 — South England & Cotswolds (Days 5–7)
Pick up the rental car. Head west.

- **Day 5:** Stonehenge (morning timed entry) + Salisbury Cathedral (Magna Carta).
  Continue to Lacock Abbey (HP classroom scenes, National Trust).
  Overnight: Cotswolds area.
- **Day 6:** Gloucester Cathedral cloisters (HP corridors). Cotswolds villages
  (Bourton-on-the-Water, Bibury, Burford — Rick Steves classics).
  Overnight: **Thornbury Castle** (Tudor, Henry VIII stayed here) if budget allows;
  otherwise a Cotswolds inn.
- **Day 7:** Oxford — Bodleian Library (HP), Christ Church College, punting on the Cherwell.
  Head north toward Wales in the afternoon.

### Block 3 — Wales (Days 8–9)
Enter Wales via the Wye Valley or A5.

- **Day 8:** Conwy Castle and walled town (one of Europe's finest medieval castles).
  Caernarfon Castle optional if energy allows. Check into **Ruthin Castle** for 2 nights
  — medieval banquet on arrival evening.
- **Day 9:** Snowdonia National Park — hike, views, fresh air. Recovery day with scenery.
  Return to Ruthin Castle for a calm evening.

### Block 4 — Liverpool & Northwest (Days 10–11)

- **Day 10:** Drive to Lydiate. Walk the village, visit Lydiate Abbey ruins,
  have a pint at The Scotch Piper Inn (~1320, oldest thatched pub in Lancashire).
  Afternoon: Liverpool — Albert Dock, Liverpool Cathedral, waterfront.
- **Day 11:** Lake District — Windermere, Ambleside, Langdale valley hike.
  Good food evening in Ambleside or Grasmere.

### Block 5 — Northeast England (Days 12–13)
This corridor has the highest concentration of HP locations outside London.

- **Day 12:** York — The Shambles (Diagon Alley inspiration), York Minster,
  city walls walk. Optional: North Yorkshire Moors Railway to Goathland
  (Hogsmeade Station) — adds ~3 hrs but the kids will love it.
- **Day 13:** Alnwick Castle (HP Hogwarts exterior, broomstick training)
  + Alnwick Gardens (Poison Garden, Treehouse Restaurant for lunch).
  Drive to Durham. Check into **Lumley Castle** (14th century, best-value castle hotel
  on the list). Medieval Black Knight banquet if available.

### Block 6 — Scotland (Days 14–19)
~6 days. Drive north from Durham.

- **Day 14:** Drive Durham → Edinburgh via the Scottish Borders (scenic).
  Check into **Dalhousie Castle** (~9 miles south of Edinburgh). Dungeon restaurant.
  Settle in, falconry walk if time allows.
- **Day 15:** Edinburgh — Edinburgh Castle, Royal Mile, Palace of Holyroodhouse,
  Greyfriars Kirkyard (HP inspiration — Tom Riddle's grave is here).
- **Day 16:** Drive Edinburgh → Fort William via Stirling Castle, Loch Lomond,
  Glencoe (stop for the view — one of Scotland's most dramatic landscapes).
- **Day 17:** **Jacobite Steam Train** — Fort William to Mallaig and back.
  Cross Glenfinnan Viaduct. Mallaig: fresh seafood lunch. Full day, leisurely.
- **Day 18:** Glenfinnan Viaduct viewpoint walk (see it from above, different perspective
  from the train crossing). Drive south toward departure point.
- **Day 19:** Buffer / travel day. Fly home from Edinburgh or Glasgow.

## Advisory: What Changes With the 2/3 South Structure

### Islay — Significantly Compromised

**The honest assessment:** Islay is very hard to do justice with only 6 Scotland days.

The logistics:
- Drive Edinburgh/Glasgow area → Kennacraig ferry terminal: ~2.5–3 hours
- Ferry to Port Ellen or Port Askaig: ~2 hours each way
- Minimum worthwhile island stay: 2 nights (to tour 3–4 distilleries properly)
- That's **4 of your 6 Scotland days** consumed by Islay alone, leaving only 2 days for
  Edinburgh and the Highlands — which is not enough for either.

**Options:**
1. **Fly Islay** — Glasgow → Islay is ~45 min by air (Loganair). Cuts the ferry out.
   Expensive for 5 people (expect £100–150pp each way) but saves a full day.
   Still costs 2–3 Scotland days. Tight but possible if Edinburgh is trimmed to 1 day.
2. **Save Islay for a future trip** — The honest Rick Steves answer. Islay deserves its
   own dedicated long weekend trip. Trying to squeeze it in does it a disservice.
3. **Mainland distillery substitute** — Auchentoshan (near Glasgow) or Glengoyne
   (Campsie Hills, 30 min from Glasgow) are excellent and add no ferry time.
   Not the same as Islay, but a genuine distillery experience that doesn't break the schedule.

**Recommendation:** Unless you fly Islay, drop it from this trip and return specifically
for it. The Jacobite train and Glencoe are the stronger experiences for a family of 5.

### Lake District — One Day Is Light

One day gives you a taste of the Lake District but not a proper experience.
If hiking is genuinely important, consider adding a day here and trimming one London day
(the city can be heavy and exhausting — Day 4 Richmond is already London-adjacent).

### Oxford vs. Bodleian

Oxford is worth a half-day minimum. If combined with Stonehenge and Lacock in one day,
it will be rushed. Consider:
- Stonehenge + Salisbury on Day 5 (standalone, it's a full morning)
- Oxford + Lacock on Day 6, Gloucester + Cotswolds villages on Day 7
That spreads the south more comfortably.

### Wales — One Castle Is Better Than Two Rushed

Conwy + Caernarfon + Ruthin in two days is full. If the pace feels tight, drop Caernarfon
and spend more time in Snowdonia. The scenery matters as much as the monuments.

## What Works Well With This Structure

- **All London must-haves** are fully achievable (Warner Bros, Platform 9¾, Ted Lasso,
  Leadenhall Market)
- **Stonehenge + Lacock + Gloucester** form a natural south-heading corridor on Day 5–6
- **Wales** fits naturally between the Cotswolds and Liverpool
- **The HP Northeast corridor** (York, Alnwick, Durham) sits perfectly as the transition
  between England and Scotland — it's on the way regardless of route
- **Lumley Castle + Durham Cathedral** is the ideal same-day combination
- **Edinburgh + Jacobite + Glencoe** is a genuinely great 6-day Scotland block
  even without Islay

## Dietary Note

Father is gluten intolerant. UK allergen laws require restaurants to disclose on request.
M&S Food halls (everywhere) have the best GF range for picnic lunches and snacks.
Notify all castle hotels at booking — they handle it without issue at this level.
Distilled whisky is generally celiac-safe; confirm with each distillery on Islay
(or wherever visited).
