# Britain 2026 — Trip Brief

## Overview

A family of 5 (Father 57, Mother 44, Daughter 19, Daughter 14, Son 14) spending
approximately 20 days in the British Isles in June–July 2026.

**Structure:**
- **Days 1–2:** Windsor (2 nights — June 28–29, first stop from Heathrow)
- **Days 3–8:** Southern England countryside (5 nights — Bradford-on-Avon, June 30–July 4)
- **Days 9–14:** Scotland (6 nights + 1 Glasgow bridge night — July 5–11)
- **Days 15–19:** London (5 nights — VRBO, July 12–16)

**Fly in:** SEA → LHR (London Heathrow), June 28
**Fly out:** LHR → SEA, July 17
**To Scotland:** Train from Bath Spa → London Paddington → King's Cross → Edinburgh Waverley (July 5)
**Car rental:**
  - Rental 1: LHR pickup June 28 → Bath Spa drop July 5
  - Rental 2: Edinburgh pickup July 5 → Glasgow Airport drop July 11

Travel philosophy: Rick Steves / Samantha Brown — thoughtful, experience-first,
not rushed. Calm evenings are non-negotiable. One great thing per day is enough.

## Block 1 — Windsor (Days 1–2, June 28–29)

Arrive Heathrow → Windsor is 20 minutes by car or taxi. Perfect first stop.
Windsor is relaxed, walkable, and a genuine decompression landing point after a transatlantic flight.

**Day 1 (June 28) — Arrive, recover**
- Pick up rental car at Heathrow; drive to Windsor (~20 min)
- Check into Airbnb (June 28–29, booked) — walkable to Windsor Castle
- Easy Thames-side walk, pub dinner, early bed
- The Long Walk (3-mile avenue from Windsor Castle to the park) is a gentle
  first-day stroll if energy allows

**Day 2 (June 29) — Windsor Castle + Marlow dinner**
- Morning: Windsor Castle (State Rooms, St George's Chapel — burial place of 10 monarchs
  including Henry VIII; one of England's finest Gothic chapels). Book at rct.uk.
- Afternoon: Eton College walk across the bridge — beautiful and free
- Evening: Drive to Marlow (~10 miles along the Thames, 20 min) for dinner with friends

**Day 3 (June 30) — Warner Bros. Studio Tour + Arrive Bradford**

Warner Bros. Studio Tour is in Leavesden, near Watford — only 30 minutes from Windsor.
**Booked: June 30, 14:00 entry.**

- Morning: Relaxed exit from Windsor (14:00 entry allows a leisurely morning). Late checkout or café breakfast.
- Drive to Leavesden (~30 min via M25/M1). Arrive ~13:30; lunch at the studio café before entry.
- 14:00: Warner Bros. Studio Tour — Great Hall, Diagon Alley sets, Hogwarts model,
  Butterbeer in the backlot. Allow 4–5 hours. Tour ends ~18:00–19:00.
- Evening: Drive M4 west to Bradford-on-Avon (~1.5 hours). Arrive ~19:30–20:30.
- Check in; grocery run (M&S or Waitrose for GF stock); easy first evening.

---

**Day 4 (July 1) — Avebury + Prehistoric Landscape**

A gentle first full countryside day. Avebury is only 30 min from Bradford.

- **Avebury Stone Circle** — Europe's largest prehistoric stone circle; the village is
  built inside it. Walk freely among the stones (no barriers, no timed entry).
  National Trust; free. More atmospheric than Stonehenge for many visitors.
- **Silbury Hill** — 5-min drive; roadside viewpoint. Largest prehistoric man-made mound
  in Europe (~2,400 BC).
- **West Kennet Long Barrow** — 5-min drive + 10-min walk. 5,500-year-old burial chamber
  you can walk inside.
- Lunch at The Red Lion, Avebury — the only pub in the world inside a stone circle
- Afternoon: Return to Bradford; quiet village wander; calm evening in

---

**Day 5 (July 2) — Stonehenge + Salisbury + Lacock Village**

- Morning: **Stonehenge** (timed entry; book at english-heritage.org.uk)
  Inner Circle option (~£50–55/person; very limited; book with flights)
- Midday: **Salisbury Cathedral** — England's tallest spire; one of four surviving 1215 Magna Cartas
- Afternoon: **Lacock village** — entirely National Trust-owned; almost no modern signage;
  arrive late afternoon when coach tours have gone

---

**Day 6 (July 3) — Bath**

Full day. It earns it.

- Roman Baths (allow 2 hours; timed entry at romanbaths.co.uk)
- Pump Room (adjacent; morning coffee or lunch in Georgian splendour; GF-friendly)
- Royal Crescent and The Circus (Georgian architecture at its finest; free to walk)
- Pulteney Bridge and Parade Gardens
- Dinner in Bath — strong GF options throughout. Return to Bradford (20–25 min).

---

**Day 7 (July 4) — Wells + Glastonbury**

The last full countryside day. Quiet and beautiful.

- **Wells Cathedral** — England's smallest city; extraordinary scissor arches.
  Vicars' Close (oldest complete medieval street in Europe) attached. Allow 1.5 hours.
- **Glastonbury Tor** — 20-min climb; ruined St Michael's Tower at the summit;
  views across the Somerset Levels extraordinary on a clear July day. Free.
- **Glastonbury Abbey ruins** — Arthurian legend; supposed burial of King Arthur.
- **Glastonbury High Street** — alternative, colourful, great for lunch
- Return to Bradford for a calm last countryside evening

**Thornbury Castle dinner option:** Thornbury is 35 min from Bradford. If they can
accommodate your group for dinner (even without a stay), the Tudor dining room is worth
booking. thornburycastle.co.uk

**Bourton-on-the-Water alternative:** If the family prefers a lighter last day,
Bourton (45 min north) makes a lovely afternoon — River Windrush, stone footbridges,
quintessential Cotswolds.

---

**Day 8 (July 5) — Drive to York**

Check out Bradford VRBO. Same rental car continues north — no car drop needed.

- Drive Bradford-on-Avon → York (~3.5 hours via A46/M1/A1(M))
- Arrive York; check into home (booked for the week)
- Afternoon walk: York City Walls or first look at The Shambles
- Evening: dinner in York — Stonegate and Gillygate have good options; GF widely available

🌡️ *The drive north means temperatures drop noticeably — Yorkshire in July averages 20–22°C,
several degrees cooler than Wiltshire.*

## Block 3 — Yorkshire & York (Days 9–14, July 6–11)

**Base:** York home (booked) · 6 nights · Car used for day trips only

---

**Day 9 (July 6) — York City Day 1: Minster · Shambles · Jorvik**

- Morning: **York Minster** — one of the finest Gothic cathedrals in northern Europe;
  naturally cool inside; climb the tower for views over the city. Allow 1.5–2 hours.
- **The Shambles** — best-preserved medieval street in England; overhanging timber-framed buildings;
  widely said to have inspired Diagon Alley. Shambles Market adjacent.
- Afternoon: **JORVIK Viking Centre** — world-class immersive experience built over actual
  10th-century Viking excavations. Book timed entry ahead. jorvik-viking-centre.co.uk
- **York City Walls** — 3.4 miles of medieval walls; walk a section; free.
- **Clifford's Tower** — Norman keep; good city views. English Heritage.
- Evening: **Betty's Café Tea Rooms** — York institution since 1936.

🌡️ *Cooling: York Minster and Jorvik are both naturally cool. River Ouse riverside walk is
shaded and breezy on warm afternoons.*

---

**Day 10 (July 7) — National Railway Museum + Harrogate**

- Morning: **National Railway Museum**, York — world's largest; free entry. Flying Scotsman
  and Mallard (world speed record locomotive) are here. Allow 2–3 hours. railwaymuseum.org.uk
- Afternoon: Drive to Harrogate (~30 min)
- **Harrogate Turkish Baths** — restored Victorian splendour; one of the finest in England.
  Book a session in advance. harrogateturkishbaths.co.uk
- **Betty's Harrogate** — the original; slightly less queued than York
- Valley Gardens, Harrogate for a stroll

🌡️ *The Turkish Baths are the best purpose-built cooling option on the entire trip.*

---

**Day 11 (July 8) — North Yorkshire Moors Railway + Goathland + Whitby**

- Morning: Drive to Pickering (~25 min). Board the **North Yorkshire Moors Railway** steam
  train to Goathland (~45 min). Book ahead for July. nymr.co.uk
- **Goathland Station** — the actual Hogsmeade Station from Harry Potter film 1.
  Unchanged from the films; charming village to walk around.
- Afternoon: Drive to Whitby (~30 min from Goathland)
- **Whitby Abbey** — Gothic ruins on the clifftop; Bram Stoker wrote Dracula inspired by
  this view; 199 steps up from the harbour.
- Fish and chips on the harbour — gold standard. Magpie Café or Trenchers.
- Optional: Robin Hood's Bay on the drive back (tiny fishing village tucked into the cliffs)

🏊 *Swimming: Whitby beach is lifeguarded in summer. North Sea is cool (~15–16°C) —
refreshing after the abbey climb.*

---

**Day 12 (July 9) — Swinton Estate: Falconry + Meal · Fountains Abbey**

- Morning: Drive to Swinton Estate, Masham (~45 min from York)
- **Falconry experience** — book directly and well in advance. swintonpark.com
- **Lunch at Swinton Estate** — book a table when booking the falconry
- Afternoon: **Fountains Abbey & Studley Royal** (~15 min from Swinton) — UNESCO World
  Heritage Site; one of the finest ruined Cistercian abbeys in Europe in a designed water garden.
  Allow 2 hours. nationaltrust.org.uk
- Optional: **Black Sheep Brewery or Theakston's**, Masham — tours and tasting rooms

---

**Day 13 (July 10) — Yorkshire Dales: Bolton Abbey + Malham**

- Morning: **Bolton Abbey** (~45 min from York) — ruins of a 12th-century Augustinian priory
  on the River Wharfe; stepping stones; beautiful woodland walks. Free to walk the grounds.
- **River Wharfe swimming** — one of the classic Dales wild swimming spots; calm, clear,
  family-friendly at the Bolton Abbey stretch.
- Afternoon: **Malham Cove** — vast curved limestone cliff with limestone pavement at the top
  (featured in Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows). 20-min walk up.
- **Gordale Scar** — dramatic limestone gorge near Malham; one of the great landscape surprises
  in England.

🏊 *Bring towels and swimwear — River Wharfe at Bolton Abbey is one of the best family
swimming spots in northern England.*

---

**Day 14 (July 11) — Castle Howard + Last York Day**

- Morning: **Castle Howard** (~30 min from York) — one of England's great baroque houses;
  filmed for Brideshead Revisited and Bridgerton. Stunning grounds and walled garden.
  Allow 2–3 hours. castlehoward.co.uk
- Afternoon: Final York hours — Yorkshire Museum and gardens, riverside walk along the Ouse,
  or revisit favourite spots from the week.
- Evening: Farewell Yorkshire dinner. Drop rental car at York — LNER to London tomorrow.

Check into London VRBO (July 12–16). London by Tube and bus; no car needed.

## Block 4 — London (Days 15–19, July 12–16)

5 nights in a VRBO. South or west London recommended (Battersea, Clapham, Putney, Fulham).
WB Studio Tour done on Day 3 — London has 5 full days free for everything else.

---

**Day 15 (July 12) — Arrive London + Settle**

- Morning: LNER York → London King's Cross (~2 hours)
- Afternoon: Check in; light walk around the VRBO neighbourhood
- Evening: Quiet local dinner; early bed

---

**Day 16 (July 13) — Richmond + Ted Lasso + The Mousetrap**

*Afternoon:*
- Richmond Park (2,500 acres; free-roaming red and fallow deer)
- Richmond Hill viewpoint (the only view in England protected by Act of Parliament)
- The Prince's Head pub, Richmond Green (the actual Crown & Anchor from Ted Lasso)

*Evening:*
- The Mousetrap, St Martin's Theatre (~7:30pm curtain). Agatha Christie; running since 1952.
  Book at delfontmackintosh.co.uk — 2–3 months ahead for July.
- Pre-theatre dinner near Covent Garden

---

**Day 17 (July 14) — Westminster + High Tea / Darts + Harrods**

*Morning (all 5 together):*
- Churchill's War Rooms, King Charles Street SW1. Allow 1.5–2 hours. iwm.org.uk
- Westminster Abbey — Coronation church; Poets' Corner; Tomb of the Unknown Warrior.
  Allow 2 hours. westminster-abbey.org
- Lunch near St James's Park

*Afternoon — The family splits:*
- **Mom + Daughters → Fortnum & Mason**, Diamond Jubilee Tea Salon, 181 Piccadilly W1.
  ~£75–85/person. Mention GF at booking. fortnumandmason.com
- **Dad + Son → Flight Club Darts**, Nova, Victoria SW1E. flightclubdarts.com

*Late afternoon — Regroup at Harrods:*
- Food halls (excellent GF), spirits department, toy hall.

---

**Day 18 (July 15) — British Museum + Tower + Leadenhall + Globe**

- Morning: **British Museum** — Rosetta Stone, Elgin Marbles, Egyptian mummies, Sutton Hoo
  helmet. Free. Arrive at opening (10am). Allow 2.5–3 hours.
- Afternoon: **Tower of London** — Crown Jewels, Beefeater tour, White Tower armour.
  Book timed entry at hrp.org.uk. Allow 2.5–3 hours.
  **Tower Bridge** walk (free, 10 min)
  **Leadenhall Market** — Victorian iron-and-glass covered market; 10-min walk
- Evening: **Shakespeare's Globe Theatre** — evening performance (~7:30pm).
  Groundling yard tickets £5; gallery seats £25–45. shakespearesglobe.com
  **Borough Market** — outstanding GF dinner options

---

**Day 19 (July 16) — Last London Day**

- **National Gallery**, Trafalgar Square (free)
- **South Bank walk** — Tate Modern, Millennium Bridge, Borough Market
- Optional swimming: **Hampstead Heath Ponds** (lifeguarded freshwater; free) or
  **Serpentine Lido**, Hyde Park (small entry fee) — perfect on a warm last day
- Quiet last dinner in London

---

**July 17 — Fly Home**

Check out VRBO. Transfer to Heathrow. LHR → SEA.

## Logistics Notes

**Car rental — one rental throughout:**
- Pick up Heathrow June 28.
- Drive Windsor → Leavesden (WB Tour June 30) → Bradford-on-Avon → York (July 5).
- Drop off York city July 11–12.
- London: No car needed. Oyster card or contactless bank card for all Tube and bus travel.

**London Tube:**
- Oyster card or contactless bank card — tap in and out, daily fare cap applies.

**Gluten free:**
- M&S Food halls: best GF ready-made range in Britain. Stock up at any branch.
- Bath, Borough Market, and Glastonbury all have strong GF options.
- Notify any castle properties at booking.

**Heat & cooling:**
- Yorkshire and York are 3–5°C cooler than the south of England in July.
- Stone buildings throughout the trip (cathedrals, castles, museums) stay naturally cool.
- Best swimming: Farleigh Swimming Club near Bradford (river); River Wharfe at Bolton Abbey;
  Whitby beach; Hampstead Heath Ponds or Serpentine Lido in London.
- Harrogate Turkish Baths (Day 10) — book in advance; the best purpose-built cooling stop.
